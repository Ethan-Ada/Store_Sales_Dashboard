This project is an interactive sales dashboard built using Power BI to explore sales performance across products, regions, and categories. The goal of this project was to practice data visualization and extract meaningful insights from a retail dataset.

Tools Used: 
-Microsoft Power BI
-Data Visualization
-DAX (basic measures)

Features:
-KPI cards showing total sales and total orders
-Sales trend over time (line chart)
-Sales by region comparison
-Top products by total sales
-Category and sub-category breakdown
-Region slicer for interactive filtering

Key Insights:

-Sales are heavily concentrated in the East region, which significantly outperforms the other regions. The Central and West regions contribute very little in comparison.
-Sales peaked in 2016, followed by a sharp drop in 2017 and a recovery in 2018. This suggests possible seasonal or external business factors affecting performance.
-Technology is the dominant category, generating the majority of total sales, while Furniture and Office Supplies contribute much less.
-Within Technology, a few sub-categories (such as accessories and machines) drive most of the revenue, indicating a strong product concentration.
-Top products contribute a large share of total sales, showing that a small number of items are responsible for a significant portion of revenue.

What I Learned:

-How to design a clean and interactive dashboard in Power BI
-How to use slicers to make reports dynamic
-How to structure visualizations to tell a clear data story
-How to identify key business insights from raw data

How to Use

1. Download the .pbix file
2. Open it in Power BI Desktop
3. Use the region slicer to filter and explore the dashboard
